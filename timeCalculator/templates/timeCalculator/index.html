THEORY

How does it work??

           To demonstrate, use as an example a distance between the earth and a sun which is approximately 149597871000m, the speed of light in the vacuum is a known constant which is approximately 299792458m/s. Having this information we just apply the formula and we will have the result in seconds.

           Δt = ΔxVm= 149597871000m/299792458m/s = 499.00478s

Warning!! It is important to remember that if you want to have the result in hours and minutes it is necessary to make a conversation taking care of the bases because our system of measurement of time works with base 60 and the calculations are in base 10, that is to say...

           Δt = 499 = 480 + 19 = 8min 19s Time that light takes from the earth to the sun.

           In other words, 480/60 = 8 minutes, the remaining 19 are seconds. If we only divide the result by 60 we will have 8,316 the first view may seem correct, but, this is the result in base 10 and not base 60, 8.316 minutes are totally different from 8 minutes and 31 seconds!

           It is also important to remember that the distances between the planets can vary, since they have elliptical orbits, so the calculation can present very different results depending on when the distances used were measured.
